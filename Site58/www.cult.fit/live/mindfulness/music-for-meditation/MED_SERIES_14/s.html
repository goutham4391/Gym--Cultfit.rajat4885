Steps
Music meditation is done by a scheduled and programmed method and the following steps mentioned below: Eliminate all the distractions like closing the door and staying in privacy, turning off all the gadgets like mobile phones and televisions so that it will not disturb you. Choose music to meditate on, which will help you relax. It can be any music that a person enjoys, and the only criteria are that it should be a slow tempo without lyrics. Fast tempo will not induce a meditation state of mind, and lyrics will distract the mind. Make sure the room is in a comfortable temperature and a quiet space. Make use of a headphone or a player to concentrate well on the music by imbibing the music well into the head. This should be essentially done as a noise-cancelling step to focus better on the music. Music plays a significant role here, and therefore one must prioritize it the most. Set the timer. If you are a beginner, do not stress yourself with longer schedules. Take it lightly, consider it a relaxing activity, and adjust the time as per your capacity levels. Start with few yoga asanas to push the mind into the right state for meditations. Relax the body with stretches. Move into a comfortable position by sitting with the legs crossed or any more comfortable position for you. At this point, it is recommended to avoid lying flat as it will put a person to sleep quickly, and therefore, he/she will not be able to benefit from the meditation. After relaxing in the position, close the eyes and breathe with the diaphragm. Take a deep breath and exhale through the mouth. Breathing is an essential part of meditation and hence make sure that you breathe deeply and slowly. Focus on the music and think about all the feelings in your body evoked by the music. The key is to feel the music. Keep doing this for several minutes. Continue redirecting your attention to the music and all your physical sensations. The goal of this is to make shut your inner voice and be you. Cease the practice once the time runs out.

What to Expect from Mindfulness In Daily Life?
The four foundations of mindfulness are mentioned below and can be practised sequentially on any given day or devote to any one of them each day. Mindfulness of the body is the awareness of the body parts rather than the entire body at once. Mind yoga classes based on this foundation trains us to observe the body part by part, and recognize that the body is not permanent and not a source of lasting happiness. This way we will reach a state of selflessness. Mindfulness of feelings refers to bodily emotions and sensations. This foundation of yoga to keep the mind calm will teach us to observe the feeling and not attach any judgment to them and we become selfless in terms of feelings. Mindfulness of mind refers to awareness or consciousness and teaches us that consciousness arises from each moment from different senses and the internal state of the mind. We learn not to attach our identity with our thoughts. Mindfulness of Dharma is also known as mindfulness of mental objects. This will teach us that everything around us exists as manifestations of reality and to practice awareness of the inter existence of all things that are temporary.
